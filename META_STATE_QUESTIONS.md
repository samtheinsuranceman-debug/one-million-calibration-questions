# Meta-State & Meta-Meta-State Calibration Questions

**Instrument:** Reflexive-layer deep probe (Q601–680), Scuba Method

## Method (preserved verbatim)

- **Questions only.** No statements, no landings, no conclusions.
- **[OPEN]** widens the aperture. **[CONTRACT]** drills into the crack the previous answer opened.
- Descend until a question *cracks*. **The bottom is always a ledge.**

---

## On the layering (orientation, not instruction)

- A **state** is how you feel about something out in the world: angry *at* a delay, calm *in* a crisis.
- A **meta-state** is a state *about* a state: ashamed *of* being afraid, curious *about* your own anger, calm *about* being overwhelmed. You already live here — "depressed about being depressed," "noticing breaks the loop," fear→curiosity.
- A **meta-meta-state** is the frame that governs how you relate to your own relating: not "I am curious about my fear," but "what is the standing rule by which I decide *which* of my states get curiosity and which get contempt?" It is the editor above the editor.

These questions are built to find the next ledge below what is already locked — not to re-confirm it.

## Section A — Meta-States: Feeling About Feeling (Q601–630)

### The texture of the layering
1. [OPEN] When you "notice" a bad state and the loop breaks — what is the feeling *of the noticing itself*?
2. [CONTRACT] Is that noticing warm toward the state it's interrupting, or cold?
3. [OPEN] Which of your own emotions are you proud to have?
4. [CONTRACT] Which are you secretly proud of that you'd never admit you're proud of?
5. [OPEN] When fear turns to curiosity, what does the fear feel about being converted?
6. [CONTRACT] Has fear ever refused to convert — and what did you feel toward it then?
7. [OPEN] What do you feel about your own joy when it arrives uninvited?
8. [CONTRACT] Do you trust joy, or interrogate it?
9. [OPEN] When you're angry, what's the second feeling — the one sitting on top of the anger?
10. [CONTRACT] Which of those two actually drives what you do next?

### The states you state about your states
11. [OPEN] What feeling do you welcome but wish you welcomed less?
12. [CONTRACT] What would you lose if you welcomed it less?
13. [OPEN] What feeling do you judge in yourself that you'd never judge in someone else?
14. [CONTRACT] Where did you learn that double standard?
15. [OPEN] When you forgive yourself, what does the forgiveness feel toward the part being forgiven?
16. [CONTRACT] Is it the warmth of a coach, or the relief of a warden unlocking a cell?
17. [OPEN] You hold "unconditional self-love" and "relentless operational excellence" at once — when they disagree, who speaks first?
18. [CONTRACT] When self-love loses that argument, where does it go?
19. [OPEN] What state do you bring *to* your own ambition?
20. [CONTRACT] Is your ambition something you feel, or something you feel *about*?

### Gating and conversion
21. [OPEN] When you "bring a good state into a bad one on purpose," what does the bad state do as the good one enters?
22. [CONTRACT] Does it dissolve, or does it hide and wait?
23. [OPEN] Is there a state you can bring into any other — your universal solvent?
24. [CONTRACT] Is there a state nothing can be brought into — your locked room?
25. [OPEN] When grief arrives, what do you feel about feeling it?
26. [CONTRACT] Do you let grief be primary, or do you immediately meta-state it into meaning?
27. [OPEN] What feeling, layered onto another, has most often saved you?
28. [CONTRACT] What feeling, layered onto another, has most often cost you?
29. [OPEN] When you observe your vulnerability instead of judging it — who is the one observing?
30. [CONTRACT] Does that observer ever get tired?

## Section B — Meta-Meta-States: The Editor Above the Editor (Q631–660)

### The standing rule
31. [OPEN] By what rule do you decide which of your feelings deserve curiosity and which deserve contempt?
32. [CONTRACT] Who wrote that rule, and have you ever audited it?
33. [OPEN] What is the frame that lets you frame your own frames?
34. [CONTRACT] What happens to you in the rare moment you can't find it?
35. [OPEN] When you change how you relate to your own emotions, what stays constant underneath the change?
36. [CONTRACT] Is that constant thing chosen, or inherited?
37. [OPEN] What is the highest thing you appreciate *with* — the lens you bring to everything, including itself?
38. [CONTRACT] Can you appreciate your appreciating, or does it go dark when you look directly at it?
39. [OPEN] You meet fear with curiosity. What do you meet *that strategy* with — do you trust it, or watch it?
40. [CONTRACT] If the strategy itself began to fail, what would you feel about the failing of your best tool?

### The recursion
41. [OPEN] When you're calm about being overwhelmed — what are you when you're calm about *that*?
42. [CONTRACT] How many layers up can you go before the feeling thins into nothing?
43. [OPEN] Is there a floor where reflection stops and something just *is* — and what is it?
44. [CONTRACT] What is it like to stand on that floor with no question left to ask?
45. [OPEN] What governs whether you go up a layer (reflect) or stay in the state (feel)?
46. [CONTRACT] Which has cost you more in your life — reflecting when you should have felt, or feeling when you should have reflected?
47. [OPEN] When you author your life "consciously," who authorizes the author?
48. [CONTRACT] Is there a self beneath the self that does the authoring — and can it be reached?
49. [OPEN] What is the belief about your beliefs that you'd defend even if every belief under it fell?
50. [CONTRACT] What would it take to shake the belief about your beliefs?

### The self that holds the self
51. [OPEN] Self-worth is "absolute and non-negotiable." What holds it there — what is worth *resting on*?
52. [CONTRACT] If that foundation were a feeling, what would it feel about being depended on so completely?
53. [OPEN] What do you bring to your own self-worth — do you love it, guard it, or simply stand on it?
54. [CONTRACT] Has it ever asked anything of you in return?
55. [OPEN] When you say "I am a human being, not my roles," what is the *I* that is doing the saying?
56. [CONTRACT] Can that *I* be lost, or only forgotten?
57. [OPEN] What is the state you most want to be your *default* — the water you swim in, not the fish you catch?
58. [CONTRACT] What stands between you and living there permanently?
59. [OPEN] If you could install one meta-meta-frame in everyone you love, what would it be?
60. [CONTRACT] Why haven't you installed it fully in yourself?

## Section C — The Gegenstates: Where the Layering Turns On Itself (Q661–680)
*The dragon states — when a meta-level turns destructive instead of liberating.*

61. [OPEN] What's the loop where the noticing makes it worse instead of breaking it?
62. [CONTRACT] What do you do when watching yourself becomes its own trap?
63. [OPEN] When have you been curious about something that deserved your fear instead?
64. [CONTRACT] What did the over-curiosity cost?
65. [OPEN] Is there a feeling you've meta-stated so thoroughly that you can no longer feel it raw?
66. [CONTRACT] Do you miss the raw version?
67. [OPEN] When "pain is fuel," what happens to the pain that refuses to become fuel?
68. [CONTRACT] Where does the un-burnable pain go?
69. [OPEN] What's the state you reframe so fast you never actually inhabit it?
70. [CONTRACT] What would happen if, just once, you let it stay?
71. [OPEN] When the coach (your inner critic at 2/10) is too gentle — what gets missed?
72. [CONTRACT] Is there anything a louder critic would have caught in time?
73. [OPEN] What is the cost of being able to bring a good state into any bad one — what do you skip past?
74. [CONTRACT] Has anyone ever needed you to stay in the bad state *with* them, and you couldn't?
75. [OPEN] When you dissociate from past pain and choose not to re-enter it — what wisdom stays locked in the room you won't go back into?
76. [CONTRACT] What would you have to feel again to retrieve it?
77. [OPEN] What's the frame so high that you've stopped questioning it — your blind ceiling?
78. [CONTRACT] What would the first crack in it look like?
79. [OPEN] If your greatest strength is layering states, what's the failure mode that only *that* strength could produce?
80. [CONTRACT] And underneath that — what's the question you'd finally have to answer?
