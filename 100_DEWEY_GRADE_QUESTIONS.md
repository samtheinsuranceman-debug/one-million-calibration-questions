## Method (preserved verbatim)

- **Questions only.** No statements, no landings, no conclusions. The instrument never tells you what you are.
- **[OPEN]** widens the aperture — invites the whole field. **[CONTRACT]** drills into the crack the previous answer opened.
- Descend until a question *cracks* — when an answer surprises you, the next question follows it down.
- **The bottom is always a ledge.** No question is final; every floor is the next room's ceiling.

These 100 deliberately avoid every axis already LOCKED. They target only the zones still dark: your **language fingerprint**, your **defining-moment history**, **Matrix 8 linguistics**, the **World / Power / Place / Time** deep matrices, **attachment**, and the **shock-response** reflex that answered "I don't know."

## Section A — The Language Fingerprint (Q501–520)
*The single highest-value gap. How to word things so they land in your idiom.*

1. [OPEN] What word do you reach for so often that other people have started to notice it?
2. [CONTRACT] When you catch yourself using it, what is it usually standing in for?
3. [OPEN] Which word, said to you, makes your jaw tighten before you can think?
4. [CONTRACT] What is the oldest memory you have of someone using that word against you?
5. [OPEN] What phrase do you use to end an argument when you've already decided you're right?
6. [CONTRACT] What does it cost the other person when you reach for that phrase?
7. [OPEN] What's a sentence you say to yourself, in your own voice, right before you do something hard?
8. [CONTRACT] Whose voice was it before it became yours?
9. [OPEN] What word do you refuse to use because it feels like a lie in your mouth?
10. [CONTRACT] What would have to be true for you to allow yourself to say it?
11. [OPEN] When you praise someone and mean it completely, what exact words come out?
12. [CONTRACT] When you praise someone and don't mean it, how does the wording change?
13. [OPEN] What metaphor do you keep returning to when you explain your own life?
14. [CONTRACT] Where does that metaphor break down, and what do you say when it does?
15. [OPEN] What's the curse word, or the absence of one, that tells people you've truly lost your composure?
16. [CONTRACT] What is the last thing to leave your speech before you go silent?
17. [OPEN] What do you call the thing you're building — the real name you use when no one's listening?
18. [CONTRACT] What name did it have before this one, and why did you retire it?
19. [OPEN] What sentence, if a stranger said it to you, would make you trust them in one breath?
20. [CONTRACT] What is the word inside that sentence doing all the work?

## Section B — The Defining-Moment History (Q521–540)
*The narrative spine. The stories the OS was built to survive.*

21. [OPEN] What is the moment your life split into a before and an after?
22. [CONTRACT] What did you believe at 4:59 that you no longer believed at 5:01?
23. [OPEN] When did you first understand that you would have to answer to no one?
24. [CONTRACT] Who was in the room — or whose absence was in the room — when you understood it?
25. [OPEN] What is the loss you have never fully told anyone the whole of?
26. [CONTRACT] Which part of it do you still leave out, even now, even here?
27. [OPEN] When did your father stop being the ceiling and start being the floor?
28. [CONTRACT] What did he do, or fail to do, that moved him?
29. [OPEN] What is the first thing you remember making that you were proud of?
30. [CONTRACT] What happened to it?
31. [OPEN] When were you last truly afraid — not nervous, afraid?
32. [CONTRACT] What did the fear turn into when it left?
33. [OPEN] What is the promise you made to yourself as a child that you are still keeping?
34. [CONTRACT] What would it take to release yourself from it?
35. [OPEN] Whose forgiveness are you still, somewhere, waiting for?
36. [CONTRACT] What would change in a single ordinary day if you received it?
37. [OPEN] What is the story you tell about yourself that you suspect isn't entirely true?
38. [CONTRACT] What is the truer version you don't tell?
39. [OPEN] What was the bottom — the rock-bottom you called "raw fuel"?
40. [CONTRACT] What did you burn first to get back up?

## Section C — Matrix 8: Linguistic & Mind-Lines (Q541–560)
*The un-administered linguistics matrix — how you reframe, deflect, and reframe again.*

41. [OPEN] When someone hands you a hard limit, what's the first reframe your mind offers?
42. [CONTRACT] What's the second reframe, the one you actually act on?
43. [OPEN] How do you turn a "no" into a "not yet" without lying to anyone?
44. [CONTRACT] When the "not yet" is also a lie, what do you do instead?
45. [OPEN] When you want to change someone's mind, do you go after the belief or the evidence beneath it?
46. [CONTRACT] What do you reach for when neither one moves?
47. [OPEN] What's the question you ask to make a person hear their own contradiction?
48. [CONTRACT] What do you do with the silence after they hear it?
49. [OPEN] When a frame is being used against you, how do you name it without escalating?
50. [CONTRACT] When naming it would escalate, what do you do?
51. [OPEN] What's the difference, for you, between a reason and an excuse?
52. [CONTRACT] Which of your own reasons have you recently caught being excuses?
53. [OPEN] When you say "that's not the real issue," how do you know what the real issue is?
54. [CONTRACT] How often are you wrong about it?
55. [OPEN] What's the smallest change in wording that has ever changed an outcome for you?
56. [CONTRACT] Could you do it again on purpose, or was it luck?
57. [OPEN] How do you tell someone something they don't want to hear so that they keep listening?
58. [CONTRACT] What's the cost when it doesn't work?
59. [OPEN] What's a belief you hold that you can argue both sides of equally well?
60. [CONTRACT] Which side do you actually live on, and how do you know?

## Section D — The World, Power & Place Matrices (Q561–580)
*Where you stand in the field of others — the deep relational and power calibration.*

61. [OPEN] When the room goes chaotic and everyone freezes, what makes you the one who moves?
62. [CONTRACT] What does it feel like in your body the half-second before you step forward?
63. [OPEN] How do you decide, in seconds, whether to lead a stranger or follow them?
64. [CONTRACT] What's the tell that flips you from one to the other?
65. [OPEN] When you meet someone with far more power than you, what do you look for first?
66. [CONTRACT] What do you do when you don't find it?
67. [OPEN] How do you treat the person clearing the table, and what does that say to you about yourself?
68. [CONTRACT] When you've treated them badly, what was happening in you?
69. [OPEN] What does it mean to you to "empower" someone — in concrete terms, not the word?
70. [CONTRACT] When have you empowered someone in a way that cost you?
71. [OPEN] When you swallow something to keep the peace, where does it go?
72. [CONTRACT] What's the longest you've ever carried one of those swallowed things?
73. [OPEN] When winning matters to you "unfortunately," what is the "unfortunately" pointing at?
74. [CONTRACT] What would you have to believe to want to win cleanly?
75. [OPEN] Whose respect do you want that you'll never ask for?
76. [CONTRACT] What would you do if they offered it unprompted?
77. [OPEN] When you walk away from toxicity to clear it, where do you walk to?
78. [CONTRACT] What's there when you arrive?
79. [OPEN] What's the boundary you've drawn that you're proudest of?
80. [CONTRACT] What's the one you keep meaning to draw and haven't?

## Section E — Time, Mortality & Legacy (Q581–590)
*The chrono-kinematics ledge — future-oriented, dissociated from past pain, death as a clarity filter.*

81. [OPEN] When you "step back" from past pain and choose not to feel it again, where do you put it?
82. [CONTRACT] Does it stay where you put it, or does it move?
83. [OPEN] You said there's "always enough" time — when did you first decide that?
84. [CONTRACT] What happens in you on the rare day it feels untrue?
85. [OPEN] When you use death as a clarity filter, what's the first thing it burns away?
86. [CONTRACT] What's the last thing standing after the filter runs?
87. [OPEN] You haven't planned a legacy yet — what's the shape of the empty space where it will go?
88. [CONTRACT] Who do you imagine standing in that space?
89. [OPEN] If your future is a road ahead and a timeline to the right, what's directly behind you?
90. [CONTRACT] How often do you turn around to look?

## Section F — Shock, Rupture & the Reflex (Q591–600)
*The one somatic reflex that answered "I don't know" — the first three seconds under sudden bad news.*

91. [OPEN] In the first second after genuinely bad news lands, does the world speed up or go silent?
92. [CONTRACT] Which is it the moment after that?
93. [OPEN] Where in your body does shock arrive first?
94. [CONTRACT] What does it do there before you've thought a single word?
95. [OPEN] When you don't yet know how you'll respond, what do you do with your hands?
96. [CONTRACT] What do your hands know that you don't yet?
97. [OPEN] Who is the first person you want to reach in a rupture, and who do you actually call?
98. [CONTRACT] Why aren't they the same person?
99. [OPEN] When everything you built could break in the next hour, what becomes suddenly, blindingly clear?
100. [CONTRACT] And underneath that — what's the question you'd finally have to answer?
